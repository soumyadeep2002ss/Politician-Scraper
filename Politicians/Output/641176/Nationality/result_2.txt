Ceuta Museum: Past, present and future
José Manuel Hita Ruiz

2017

Spanish museums have played a decisive role in the research, preservation and communication of cultural heritage. In Ceuta, although previous attempts are known, the City Council only decided to create an archeological museum in 1964. In those years the number of recovered archeological pieces had increased significantly but, since there was no local museum, the possibility that they should Being transferred to the most next provincial museum, as happened before, was felt as a dramatic loss of local cultural identity. This article briefly presents the history and current situation of the Ceuta Museum, also some future projects to deal with the new museum challenges in the 21st century. Archaeological Museum of Seville (1879-2017): 138 years of history and some more

2017 •

Manuel Camacho-Moreno

The work addresses the history of the Archaeological Museum of Seville, an institution of special relevance in the context of Spanish museum. His career and training, currently managed by the Ministry of Culture of the Junta de Andalucía, shows the incidence of the regional historical context in the current personality and travel of one of the main archaeological museums of our country. His historical analysis reveals that it is perhaps the Andalusian model that best represents the passage of the ancient "Studioli" or humanistic cabinets of the 16th century towards the public museum of the late nineteenth century, and from the antichuarismo to the consolidation of archaeological science to mid -twentieth century.

The Archaeological Museum of Córdoba and the Provincial Commission of Monuments. Memory of a relationship

2017 •

Matilde Bugella Altamirano

The intense activity deployed by the Provincial Commission of Monuments of Córdoba during the central decades of the twentieth century contrasts with the general decrease of the faculties of a key institution for the protection of Spanish heritage during the nineteenth century, but which will progressively languish during the following century. Despite a chronic scarcity of economic means and that its members, representatives of the so -called "honorary administration", did not receive any remuneration, the preserved documentation allows to verify the effort deployed by the Cordoba institution to preserve the historical and artistic heritage of the city. His intervention would also play a relevant role in the consolidation of the Archaeological Museum of Córdoba, both for the support provided in the search for a definitive headquarters for it and, especially, through actions that would significantly increase their funds. The Intense Activity Displayed by the Provincial Commission of Monuments of Córdoba During the Central Decadesof the twentieth century contrasts with the general decline of an institution that had played a key role in the protection of the Spanish heritage during the nineteenth century, before gradually fading during the next century. With a chronic shortage of financial resources and despite that its members, quintessential archetypes of the so-called «honorary administration», received no remuneration, the institution archives show it spared no effort to protect the historical and artistic heritage of the city. It also played an important role in the consolidation of the Museo Arqueológico de Córdoba, both through the support provided to the museum’s search of a permanent home and, especially, through actions that significantly increased its collections.

Boletín del Museo Arqueológico Naciona, 35

El Museo de Cuenca, un proyecto intermitente e inacabado

2017 •

Magdalena Barril Vicente

Resumen El Museo de Cuenca tuvo su origen en los restos procedentes de la Ciudad Romana de Valeria recogidos por Francisco Suay desde 1952. Creado oficialmente, con carácter provincial, en 1963 por la Dirección General de Bellas Artes en régimen de Patronato con las Administraciones locales, en 1974 se trasladó a su actual sede donde se instaló de acuerdo con las innovadoras corrientes museográficas del momento. Desde entonces ha sufrido diversas vicisitudes y está pendiente de una gran remodelacióny renovación. Abstract The Cuenca Museum was born in the remains from the Roman City of Valeria gathered by Francisco Suay since 1952. Was officially created, with provincial character, in 1963, by the General Direction of Fine Arts (Heritage), with and administration together with local authorities. In 1974 moved to its current headquarters, where it settled in accordance with current new museological trend at the time. Since then it has suffered several vicissitudes and is awaiting a major refurbishment and renovation.
