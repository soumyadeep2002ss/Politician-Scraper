Location of Church of San Dionisio in Spain

The Church of San Dionisio (Spanish: Iglesia de San Dionisio) is a Church Located in Jerez de la Frontera, Andalusia, Spain. It was Built in the late 15th century in Gothic-Mudéjar Style, Although its interior was later renovated in new style (18th century) by architects Diego Antonio Díaz and Pedro de Silva. It was declared bien de interés cultural in 1964.[Citation need]

Description[edit]

The Parish Was Established by Alfonso X the Wise in the Name of Saint Denis as the City was Returned to Christian Rule on Saint Denis's Day.

The church has a basilica plan, divided into three naves by Tall and simple pillars Adorers Adorers with Almohad Decorations. The Arcades (Aside from That Near the High Altar) are Ogival. The Naves End with Apses with new altars, included the high altar which dates to the pre-new renovation.

The side chapels are in baroque style. The Chapel of the Christ of the Water Includes An Image of Jesus from the 15th Century. The tower knowing as Torre de la Ataya was also built in the Fifteenth Century. Although this is attached to the church it was a civilian construction intended to serve as a Watchtower for both fires and Attack and to hold the town's clock. The tower Has been separately listed from the church as being of cultural interest. The tower was first mention 1447 and the clock was in 1454 and the tower was first used as a Watchtower in 1484.
