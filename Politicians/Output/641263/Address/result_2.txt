Headquarters

Tags: CGPJ, Judicial School, CENDOJ

The CGPJ currently has four public buildings that serve as headquarters. Two of them are in Madrid, one in Barcelona and the other in Donostia-San Sebastián.

Headquarters

Located on Marqués de la Ensenada street, 8 (28004-Madrid). It was inaugurated on September 13, 1990 by S.M. King Juan Carlos I.

The CGPJ previously occupied two provisional venues. A first, in the building that houses the National Court (García Gutiérrez street number 1 in Madrid), where the council was until September 1981. A second on the Paseo de la Havana Numbers 140-142 of Madrid, where the CGPJ remained until Your definitive transfer to your current location.

Citizen Attention and Continuous Training

Located on Trafalgar 27-29, Madrid, this second headquarters houses the continuous training service of the Judicial School, the Inspection Service and the Citizen Attention Office.

Judicial School

It is located on a farm farm of Collserola, called "La Esperança", in the old municipality of San Gervasi de Cascoles, today in Barcelona. This singular installation has three clearly identified parts. A first, on an existing volume, which has been allocated, following a careful rehabilitation, to offices for the direction and administration of the Judicial School, as well as the library. The second, again, houses the classrooms and the auditorium. The third, also again, is intended for common services and communicates the previous two. The farm is owned by the generality of Catalonia that gave its use to the General State Administration to locate the Judicial School of the CGPJ for a period of thirty years.

Judicial Documentation Center (CENDOJ)

Located at the Palace of the Provincial Court of San Sebastián, on San Martín Street. It is a classic building, inaugurated in 1916, of rectangular plant and built with silry stone. It is the work of the architect Donostiarra José Gurruchaga y Martiarena, who opted for the domain of open hollow spacious, large stained glass and straight lines. The Government of the Autonomous Community of the Basque Country has given its use free of charge to the CGPJ, for fifty years to house the headquarters of CENDOJ.
