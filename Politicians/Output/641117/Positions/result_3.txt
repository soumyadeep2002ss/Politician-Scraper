Description
Luis Miguel de Camps García Executive President of the Dominican Association of Fiduciary Societies, Inc. (ASOFIDOM) from 2014 to 2020. He has been politically active for many years, currently being the president of the Social Revolutionary Democratic Party (PRSD).

On August 16, 2020, Lic. De Camps García was appointed as Labor Minister of the Dominican Republic. On the occasion of this designation, he paused his private exercise as long to his new tasks as a public servant.

Professional career and work experience:
Luis Miguel de Camps García began his professional career in New York City, United States of America, as an international lawyer at Fox Horan & Camerini LLP (2003-2005), where he specialized in corporate structures, international financial regulations and The United States of America, patrimonial planning, design and implementation of related tax structures, international transactions, immigration regulations and intellectual property issues in general.
Upon returning to the Dominican Republic, he founded the law firm of Camps, Vásquez and Valera. He has worked with international financial institutions, promoters and operators of commercial and residential real estate, as well as with corporate clients, in general matters related to business operations in the Dominican Republic, and in specialized matters such as mergers, acquisitions, joint ventures, and transactions local and international financial.
In 2014 he was appointed Executive President of the Dominican Association of Fiduciary Societies, Inc. (ASOFIDOM); position he occupied for a period of six years (2014-2020). Through Decree 324-20 dated August 16, 2020, he was appointed as Labor Minister of the Dominican Republic.

Education and recognition:
Lic. De Camps García completed his law degree at the Pontifical Catholic University Mother and Teacher (PUCMM) with academic honors (2001). He also completed Master's studies at Georgetown University in Washington, DC, United States of America, Masters of Law (LL.M) with specialization in business law, and postgraduate studies at Harvard University, Massachusetts, United States of America, with Specialization in negotiation and conflict resolution.

Membersies and teaching positions:
Member of the Bar Association of the Dominican Republic. Member of the National Association of Young Entrepreneurs. Referee of the Center for the Alternative Dispute Resolution of the Chamber of Commerce and Production of Santo Domingo (2007 - Present). Professor of Law of Securities and Guarantees, Pontifical Catholic University Mother and Teacher (PUCMM) (2007 - Present). Professor of Introduction to Law, Pontifical Catholic University Mother and Teacher (PUCMM) (2006).
He was a panelist about Trust at the Latin American Congress of Fidecomise (Colafi), held in Cartagena, Colombia (2018), and has actively participated in national seminars and panels specialized in trusts.

PUBLICATIONS AND NEWS:
Mr. Camps García is the author of articles on law and economy in a variety of publications, such as newspapers and RSpecialized business evidence, both from the point of view of business and from the perspective of the socioeconomic impact of regulations.
