Buildings that currently suppose a contemporary patrimonial collection that inserted, either in the historical cities or being part of their growth, make up the cultural legacy of the same along with other buildings of greater historical route. At present, the evolution experienced by the concept of heritage promotes the appearance of numerous types of intrinsic values to a patrimonial good beyond historical and artistic value. Issue by which the consideration of modern architecture arises as contemporary heritage. Given that currently the use of a property is part of the patrimonial value of a good, the changes of uses and adaptations of these elements to the needs of the 21st century, although they are presented as an opportunity to keep heritage active, They could suppose the loss of their patrimonial values. Patrimonial protection, through urban planning figures, delimits the type of interventions that can legally be carried out on this heritage, as well as their scope. Highlight, that the least protection that these properties usually have with respect to those of greater antiquity, sharpens the possible loss of previously indicated values. To address this analysis, buildings that were built in the period between 1925 and 1975 in Seville are taken, and still preserved. This is intended to show what were the uses that transformed the historical city, decade to decade through architecture, as well as those that would characterize the new urban growth. Elements that are erected today as the contemporary heritage legacy of the Andalusian city.
