Inés María Cañizares Pacheco (Las Pedroñeras, Cuenca, September 27, 1970) is a Spanish economist and politic

Biography [edit]

Born in the municipality of Las Pedroñeras3 within a working family. His father was a seller of agricultural machinery, and subsequently employee of banking; His mother was a hairdresser.4

After graduating in Business Sciences at the University of Castilla-La Mancha (1993) he conducted a master's degree in Superior Specialization in Economic and Financial Accounting Audit from the Institute of Business Administration (IADE) at the Autonomous University of Madrid (1994). It is an account auditor, belonging to the Institute of Censors of Accounts of Spain (ICJCE) .5

Cañizares developed his professional activity by establishing his professional office as an economist.6

During the general elections held in November 2019, he held second place in the list of the constituency of Toledo for the Congress of Deputies, being elected.7 In Congress, he is a member of the Permanent Diputación; of the Territorial Policy Commission; of the monitoring and evaluation commission of the agreements of the Toledo Pact; Member of the Finance Commission and the Budget Committee; Deputy spokesman of the Board of Spokespersons; of the Budget Commission; of the Commission of Economic Affairs and Digital Transformation; and of the mixed commission for relations with the Court of Accounts.1

He replaced Macarena Olona as an attached spokesman for the party in Congress. In the municipal elections of May 2023, the City of Toledo was presented as head of the list.8
