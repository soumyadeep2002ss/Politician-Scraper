10 Most Spoken Languages in India by Number of Speakers
After searching on Google about the "ugliest language in India", the search result displays "Kannada" which is a language spoken by around 40 million people in South India. First, it was seen on debtconsolidationsquad.com. India is home to various major languages. Let us read about the search and 10 most spoken languages in India, according to the 2011 census of languages.    
By SHIKHA GOYAL
FEB 21, 2024, 12:16 IST
10 most spoken languages in India

If you search on Google about the "ugliest language in India" the search result displays is shocking and the answer displayed is Kannada which is one of the oldest languages in the world. It was first seen on debtconsolidationsquad.com.

Because of this, there is an outrage on social media. People are demanding that Google should apologise and take strict action against it. An IT company named Thincnext in Bangalore has started a petition on charge.org to remove the search result from Google. 

To know more about it read What is the 'Ugliest Language in India' Controversy?

The most pivotal feature of human existence is the language that helps humans to communicate with each other and develop relations between them.

We know that India is the world’s seventh-largest country by area as well as the world's second-most populous country. The country has a population of more than 1.3 billion people. There are several religions practiced in the country. It consists of diverse languages and cultures and people speaking different languages are scattered in different parts of the country.

Do you know that there are 121 languages that are spoken by 10,000 or more people in India, which has a population of more than 121 crores, according to a census analysis? More than 19,500 languages or dialects are spoken in India as mother tongue, according to the analysis of a census. Let us find out the most spoken languages and speakers in the country as per 2011 Census.

1. Hindi - 52.83 crore speakers

Hindi is the most spoken language in India. It is one of the official languages of the country. According to the 2011 census, the number of people who speak Hindi as a mother tongue has increased in 2011 as compared to the 2001 census. In 2001, 41.03% of the people speak Hindi as mother tongue, while in 2011 it is increased to 43.63%. After Mandarin, Spanish and English, Hindi is also the fourth most spoken language in the world. At present, there are 52, 83, 47,193 Hindi speakers in India. It is spoken in almost all the states of the country including Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, Uttarakhand, Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh, Chhattisgarh, Himachal Pradesh and Delhi.

Hindi is a descendant of the Sanskrit language and is influenced by Dravidian, Arabic, Portuguese, English, Persian, and Turkey languages. Languages spoken in Hindi include Awadi, Braj, and khadi bhasha. Did you know that the Hindi word is divided into five categories, such as Tatsama Ardhatasama, Tadbhav, Deshaj, and Videshi?

2. Bengali - 9.72 crore speakers

India's second most spoken language is Bengali, which is spoken by 9.72 crore citizens i.e. 8.03 percent of the total population.
