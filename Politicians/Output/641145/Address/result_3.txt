María José García-Pelayo Jurado was born in Jerez de la Frontera on January 6, 1968. Bachelor of Law from the University of Cádiz. Married and with a daughter.

Politics and lawyer, is mayor of Jerez since June 17, 2023, a position he has for the third time after being in 2003 (June 2003 - January 2005) and in 2011 (June 2011 - June 2015). She is currently president of the Spanish Federation of Municipalities and Provinces.

Councilor of the City of Jerez from 1995 to 2019, provincial deputy and spokesman of the Popular Group in the Diputación in the 1999-2003 Legislature, Vice President of the Andalusian Parliament (2008-2011) and Autonomic Deputy.

In Congress he has occupied the vice presidency of the Agriculture Commission and has been a member of the Equality, Defense and spokesperson commissions of Culture.

She is a local president of the PP of Jerez de la Frontera. President of the PP of Cádiz between 1999 and 2004, has been part of the National, Regional and Provincial Executive Committee.

Biography
He was born on October 9, 1966 in Granada. He conducted his ecclesiastical studies at the San Ildefonso de Toledo Major Seminary between 1985-1987 and 1989-1992. During these years he was formed priestly at the Seminar for Adult Vocations Santa Leocadia de Toledo, where between 1987 and 1989 he carried out a course of spirituality and another of ecclesiastical languages.
He has a degree in Dogmatic Theology (1994) and Doctor of Patristic Theology (1998) from the Gregorian Pontifical University of Rome.


Pastoral charges
He has developed his priestly ministry in Granada and Toledo. He was a parochial vicar of the “Corpus Christi” parish of Granada and consiliario of the Private Association of faithful Cultural and Social Action (ACYS) between 1994 and 1995.
In 1996 he began his teaching activity as a ordinary professor at the Institute of Spiritual Theology of Barcelona from 1996 to 2011. From 1996 to 1998 he served as auxiliary professor at the Faculty of Theology, from the Gregorian Pontifical University of Rome. From 1998 to 2012 he was a professor of dogmatic theology at the San Ildefonso de Toled Patristic theme. He has also been a guest professor at the Leo XIII Institute of Social Doctrine of the Church of Madrid and in the Faculty of Theology of the University of Navarra. He was pastor of the parish of Our Lady of Purification of Nambroca (Toledo) between 1998 and 2001; and priest assigned to the parish of Santo Tomé de Toledo, from 2001 to 2012. In the Archdiocese of Toledo he has also been director of the Secretariat for the doctrine of faith and ecumenism, from 2005 to 2011. From 2001 to 2013 he has been director of the Secretariat of the Episcopal Commission for the Doctrine of Faith, of the Spanish Episcopal Conference. From 2001 to 2012 it has belonged to the Association of Patrologists of the East and West, of the Pro Oriente Foundation (headquarters in Vienna). And currently belongs to the Scientific Committee of the “Patristos Fuentes” collection of the Editorial Ciudad Nueva.
He was appointed Auxiliary Bishop of Getafe for Benedict XVI on July 6, 2012 and received the Episcopal Consecration on September 21, 2012.
Appointed bishop of Asidonia-Jerez on June 9, 2021
