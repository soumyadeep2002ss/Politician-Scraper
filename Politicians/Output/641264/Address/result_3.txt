Enrique Gil Botero takes possession as the new general secretary of the COMJIB

Today, Enrique Gil Botero, former Minister of Justice of Colombia, took possession, as the new general secretary of COMJIB for the period between 2019 and 2023.

September 17, 2019

The Colombian Enrique Gil Botero took possession today, September 17, as the new general secretary of the Conference of Justice of the Ibero -American countries (COMJIB), the international body that groups the Ministries of Justice and homologous institutions of the 21 countries of The Ibero -American Community and that aims to study and promote forms of legal cooperation between Member States.

The new secretary general was accompanied by the Ibero -American Secretary, Rebeca Grynspan; the Minister of Justice of Spain, Dolores Delgado; and the Secretary of State for International Cooperation and for Latin America and the Caribbean of Spain, Juan Pablo de Laiglesia.

Secretary Grynspan welcomed Gil Botero reiterating Segib's support for the agency and stressing that the Ibero -American space shares a common legal culture, in addition to values and principles.

For her part, Minister Delgado reiterated the support of Spain to Secretary Gil and advocated the construction of fairer societies by the Comjib, with the commitment to continue building in Latin America a legal system of the 21st century.

Gil Botero has a solid career as a conjugation of the Constitutional Court of Colombia and the Superior Council of the Judiciary, president of the State Council and magistrate of the Third Section of said Corporation between 2006 and 2014; Commissioner of the Inter -American Commission on Human Rights between January 2016 and March 2017 and more recently as Minister of Justice and Law of the Republic of Colombia between March 2017 and August 2018.

Comjib is part of the Strategic Management Committee of Ibero -American Organizations (CODEI), in which the organization of Ibero -American States for Education, Science and Culture (OEI), the Ibero -American Organization for Social Security (OISS) participate, the organization International Youth for Latin America (OIJ) and the Ibero -American General Secretariat (SEGIB).
