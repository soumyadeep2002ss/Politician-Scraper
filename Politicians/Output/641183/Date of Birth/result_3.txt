Roquetas de Mar is a Spanish town and municipality of the province of Almería and the Autonomous Community of Andalusia, capital of the homonymous judicial party, located in the region of the Almeria west and 21 kilometers from the provincial capital, Almería. In 2023, it had 110,416 inhabitants and was the second municipality of the province behind the capital and 61.º of Spain in terms of population. It holds the title of Villa.

Toponymy [edit]

By the year 1508 it could be found in the cartography indicated as Lena D'Almería, although his current name arose very shortly after.3

Although its origin is not very specified, it is believed that its most successful base can be the common name romance roca happened by the diminutive suffix "-eta" .4

In the book of Apero and Distribution of the Taha de Felix, 1573, the beach of which we know as Castillo de Santa Ana as “Las Roquetas” appears. Throughout the cartography of the seventeenth century onwards, this enclave such as La Roqueta or Las Roquetas will always be appointed. On other occasions such as “El Castillo de las Roquetas” .5 From the year 1737 the first settlers begin to settle, in the area of the castle of Santa Ana. Mainly they came from the Sierra de Gádor specifically from the current Felix, Enix And I saw.

The diminutive suffix that possesses "-the" denounces its probable Catalan or Valencian origin. The well -known continued presence of Catalan sailors6 in this part of the coast comes to favor or confirm that they were surely those who created the place name. In fact, in Catalan some "roquetes" are a portion of low agricultural quality and is relatively frequent as a place name.

The "Mar" denomination was granted by Royal Decree on July 2, 1916, to avoid confusion with Roquetas, in the province of Tarragona.

At the beginning of the 18th century there are the first lasting settlements with neighbors of Enix or Felix, once the coastline will be considered safe from the attacks of the Berber pirates. In 1737 some farmhouses, the town and houses of the military garrison in the surroundings of the Castle of Santa Ana, whose denomination was the Roquetas, the coast of the Kingdom of Granada were divided into districts and in 1739 the district of Almería was It understood from Roquetas de Mar to Mesa Roldán, being the Alcara de Almería the central strength. Roquetas de Mar was considered a square of great military interest although it failed to establish itself as such, thus leaving its activity relegated to the salt and fishing. In 1776 Roquetas was segregated by Felix and constituted as a municipality. In 1782 the demarcations of the municipality are made and the main occupations of the inhabitants are the military, fishing and agriculture.22

During the nineteenth century the neighborhoods of the port and Cortijos de Marín. Agriculture and fishing were the main means of subsistence. In 1954 the National Institute of Colonization developed the extension of Roquetas de Mar. following the same type of urban planning of all colonization projects, numerous white houses, the school, the houses of teachers, social buildings and the administrative building and the administrative building were built . The architect was José García-Niet Gascón and the project included 154 houses. Its most outstanding element is the closed rectangular geometry colonization square with a porticued facade. It is currently Mayor José Pomares Martínez. Within the actions of the National Institute of Colonization, the populations of El Parador de las Hortichuelas (1954), El Solanillo (1968) and Las Marinas (1958) are also developed. The homes are built with both housing space and for agricultural units, in two floors, with barn and hay ventilated by lattices. These peoples were copies in terms of their urbanism, of rational layout and seeking the socialization of the settlers. Vanguard architectural solutions were applied and designed for self -sufficiency. The settlers came from the Alpujarras and the rest of the province of Granada.28 29 30 31

Civil [edit]
Sea Roquetas lighthouse: Built in 1863, it was acquired at the end of the 20th century by the City of Roquetas, becoming an exhibition hall.
Torre de la Molineta: Antigua Molineta for obtaining water built in the middle of the twentieth century. It is of circular plan topped by battlements and covered with lime mortar.57
The trace, primal buildings of homes and services and churches of the villages of colonization of El Parador, Roquetas de Mar, Las Marinas and El Solanillo.
Buildings in modern style linked to development as a tourist town of the Almería coast of the late twentieth century: Chapel Our Lady of the Winds (1972) in urbanization of Roquetas; Los Balandros, El Palmeral (Aguadulce), El Faro, etc.58

Religious [edit]
Church of Our Lady of the Rosary (18th century) whose main altarpiece is the work of the Indalian artist Jesús de Perceval.
Our Lady of the Winds Chapel: built in 1972 with the works of creation of the urbanization of Roquetas, is located in the middle of an landscaped area, which dominates it. The chapel, built in concrete, consists of an altar, with a flat cover, as well as a series walls that protect it. It stands on a concrete platform that makes it above the banks that find them around to be used by parishioners.59
Hermitage of the Holy Cross. Chapel of the early twentieth century located in Avda. De Juan Bonachera.
Intangible Cultural Heritage [edit]

The various population centers of the municipality of Roquetas de Mar as well as some of their neighborhoods, have their own patron saint parties that are held practically during all years.

Of all of them that have a higher influx of public and with greater tradition, it is those of the Virgen del Carmen in Aguadulce, San Isidro Labrador in the Hortichuelas Parador, Santa Ana in El Puerto, Virgen del Rosario in Roquetas Centro and Virgen del Mar in marinas.

In all of them, as in the rest of the parties of the province of Almería, the "noon fair" is celebrated during the day, where you can enjoy the tapas and have fun at the same time, in the enabled enclosures. And during the night the night fair, with concerts, markets and other activities.

The election of the Patroness of Roquetas de Mar was held at a meeting held by the Council. The search for a pattern is due to the despair of the Roqueteros by the epidemic of the third fever suffered by the people. For the choice of the new pattern, the main images of the municipality's church and a child, Antonio Villanueva, was chosen to take one of the many images in the wave. Antonio Villanueva took the image of the Virgen del Rosario who became the crowned patron of the municipality and perpetual mayor.

In Roquetas the brotherhood of Our Lady of the Dolores processes the crucified Christ in his divine mercy followed by the Virgen de los Dolores, both in different thrones. This procession is highly anticipated by the Roqueteros and one of the most devotion causes inside and outside the Brotherhood, since the Christ was highly anticipated when he reached the Church of Ntra. Mrs. del Rosario.

On Good Friday two processional parades take place: the brotherhood of Ntra. Mrs. de los Dolores performs two processions. The first is called the Holy Burial of the Redeemer, in which the figure of the Christ Yacent in a sepulcher, followed by the Virgen de los Dolores, both in different thrones. After this procession a break of about two hours is held, where the brotherhoods change the cord for one of the same color as the tunic and the mantle is changed to Ntra. Mrs. De Los Dolores for a mourning one. The second procession is called loneliness, in which the Virgen de los Dolores comes out only. This procession is the oldest in the brotherhood (it has been carried out since 1991, that is, the year in which Ntra. Mrs. de los Dolores for the first time). It stands out for the prayer of the Rosary during the same and because the tour takes place through narrow streets.

Cultural facilities [edit]

The municipality has two museums and an auditorium where numerous cultural avivities develop: 61

CLASSROOM: It is a 500 square meter museum where the importance of the fauna and flora of the Mediterranean Sea is shown, as well as the interaction of man with the same. There are panels, videos, models and all kinds of resources to show their educational and training values.
Museum of the Bull Square: It consists of four exhibition halls.
Sea Roquetas Auditorium: Inaugurated in 2004, it has a capacity for 1,300 spectators, hosting all kinds of musical and theatrical events.

Sport [edit]

Mar Roquetas had a football team in the Second Division B of Spain, called Roquetas Sports Club. He played his home games at the Antonio Peroles Municipal Stadium, built on the occasion of the 2005 Mediterranean Games. In 2016 his disappearance occurred.

Mar Roquetas, in addition, has a sports pavilion called the Infanta Cristina pavilion. Roquetas de Mar has a basketball team in the 1st National Men's Division Club Basketball Roquetas de Mar. plays its home games in the Infanta Cristina pavilion. The Roquetas de Mar Basketball Club was founded in 1989.

In Roquetas de Mar you can also practice many nautical sports, such as diving, fishing, surfing or aquatic skiing, many of them in the Royal Yacht Club of Roquetas de Mar, located in the neighborhood of El Puerto.
