Mamata Banerjee (Bengali: মমতা বদ্যপাধাধ়) (born 5 January 1955), popully known as "Didi",[2] is an Indian politician. She became the Chief Minister of the Indian State of West Bengal on 20 May 2011. She is the first woman Chief Minister of the State. She is also the Founder and Chairperson of the Trinamool Congress.[3][4] Mamata Banerjee is also the minister for home, health and family welfare, land and land reforms, information and cultural agendas, hill affairs, minority affairs and madrassah education, agriculture, power and personnel and administrative reforms Department. 7][8] She is a good orrator. Her followers call her "reaning the elder Sister).

Mamata Banerjee has defeated the 34-year-old the Communist Party of India (Marxist)-Leed left front government in West Bengal. That government was the world's longerving Democratically-elected communist government.[9][10][10][10][11]

In the past, she was the Minister of Railways Twice, Minister of Coal Once, and Minister of State of Human Resource Development, Department of Youth Affairs Affairs and Sports and Women and Child Development Once in the Cabinet of the Government of India.
