Dr. Gina Magnolia Riaño Barón, New Secretary General of Oiss

Posted by: General Secretariat of Oiss
Elected by the Permanent Committee of OISS on November 27 in Bogota

Dr. Gina Magnolia Riaño Barón was elected Secretary General of the Ibero-American Social Security Organization, by the Permanent Committee of the Organization, by delegation of the Council of Directors, agreed at her meeting of 9 March 2012 in Montevideo (Uruguay); Who will replace Mr. Adolfo Jiménez Fernández, current secretary general of the organization.

Dr. Riaño was Minister of Labor and Social Security of Colombia during the period from 1999 to 2000 and Minister of Responsible Health. Chairman of the Board of Directors of the Institute of Social Security, Director General of the National Fund for Social Security, Director and Secretary General of the National Institute for Transport and Traffic (Intra). He also served as Judge of the Republic and Municipal Controller. She was born in Tunja, Colombia, is married and is the mother of two children.

Degree in Law. Lawyer and Specialist in Social Security from Colombia Delivered University, Master in Social Security Systems Management and Management and Social and Social Assistance Management and Management of the University of Alcalá and Oiss. Specialist in Administrative Law at Universidad del Rosario. He studied cooperativism in Tel Aviv (Israel). Diploma of the Senior Management Program of the Universidad de Los Andes.

Professor and researcher, postgraduate professor at universities: Javeriana, National of Colombia, External, Santo Tomás, Ces de Medellin. Unab de Bucaramanga, north of Barranquilla and Francisco de Vitoria da Spain. As a researcher, he directed and participated in various research and studies on public policies and performance of the region's security and social protection systems.

He chaired Colombia's delegation at the International ILO Conference in Geneva, Switzerland. He actively participated in the International Labor Conference, in which the Convention on Maternity Protection was approved in 2000 (183).

He participated as the author or co-author of various legislative initiatives in Colombia, such as the law that created the General Social Security System, the law to protect mothers from the community, the Ibero-American Social Security Code, the law that creates the bottom. Territorial level pensions and health system reform laws, pensions, labor risks and social protection in general. In addition to the laws that modified the Code of Substantive Labor and the Code of Procedure and Social Security of Colombia.

Dr. Riaño will take possession of its new position on February 1, 2014.
