The new general secretary of the OISS, Gina Magnolia Riaño Barón takes possession

Posted by: General Secretariat Oiss
In the Ibero -American Secretary (Segib) in Madrid, before the Ibero -American Secretary, Enrique Iglesias

The new general secretary of the Ibero -American Social Security Organization, Gina Magnolia Riaño, took possession in the SEGIB, in an act held in the Ibero -American General Secretary and attended of the scope of social security. The new General Secretariat, whose effective possession begins on February 1, replaces Adolfo Jiménez Fernández, who held the position for 22 years.

Gina Magnolia Riaño has been elected by the Permanent Committee of the Organization on November 27, 2013 in Bogotá, by delegation of the Board of Directors, agreed at its meeting on March 9, 2012 in Montevideo (Uruguay), she has been a minister of labor and Social Security of Colombia during the period L999-2000 and Minister of Health in charge. President of the Board of Directors of the Social Security Institute, General Director of the National Social Security Fund, Director and General Secretary of the National Institute of Transportation and Transit (Intra); She currently serves as Regional Director of the OISS for Colombia and the Andean area. He has also exercised as judge of the Republic and Municipal Personera. She was born in Tunja, Colombia, is married and is the mother of two children.

She has a law degree. Lawyer and Social Security Specialist at the Externado University of Colombia, University Master in the Management and Management of Social Security Systems and in Welfare and Social Services Management of the University of Alcalá and the OISS. Specialist in Administrative Law of the University of Rosario. He studied Cooperativismo in Tel Aviv (Israel). Diploma of the Senior Management Program of the University of Los Andes, Colombia.

In the act of possession, the outgoing secretary, Adolfo Jiménez Fermández, highlighted the professional capacities and competences of the new general secretary, stating: "The new general secretary, Gina Magnolia Riaño, in turn, has a total knowledge of the OISS" He said "on the other hand and I think it must stand out, a woman arrives at the General Secretariat, which is in line with the signs of the times and, without a doubt, will have a beneficial impact." He thanked the Spanish authorities for the Ministries of Employment and Social Security, the Minister, the Secretary of State for Social Security and the directors of the Management Entities and General Treasury of Social Security to those responsible for the collaborating entities for the Great collaboration and support they have lent to the organization. He said "this general secretariat has always received great support and cooperation from all social security administrations in Spain, interchangeably from their political orientation." Likewise, he thanked the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Cooperation and especially the AECID.

Likewise, Adolfo Jiménez, made a referencecia to the programs of the organization, to the advances and relevance of each of them in the current economic and social context, highlighting the Ibero -American Social Security multilateral agreement, on which he said: “Latin America is the second region From the world by number of migrations, this increasingly intense, diverse and complex phenomenon requires, in the same way, special attention by the states, particularly in social security matters to avoid the lack of protection and poverty of its citizens, With which the OISS proposed the initiative, developed and is developing the Ibero -American Social Security multilateral agreement, which is already a reality in its application and that protects migrant workers, their families and multinational workers; promoting, among other things, the normative coordination in the field of social protection, within the framework of respect for national social security systems of the different states of the region ”

For his part, the Ibero -American Secretary, Enrique V. Iglesias, highlighted the work of Adolfo Jiménez Fernández and said that the OISS has promoted, with the accompaniment of the SEGIB, the Ibero -American Multilateral Agreement of Social Security, said that: “It is the First citizen instrument »In the same way, he offered the new Secretary General the continuous cooperation of the SEGIB.

On the other hand, in her speech, Dr. Riaño made a mention to her link to the OISS, her journey from the beginning, the advances in Colombia on Social Security, in addition, among other issues of interest, said that: " Social security constitutes an advance of democracy and civilization, and it is our duty, as an organization, to promote adequate protection to the inhabitants of our region against this type of risks, "he also pointed out that" Social Security is a human right enshrined in the Declaration of Human Rights itself and that clause must be translated into the recognition of social security as a fundamental right, since in current constitutionalism the tendency imposed is that human rights are supra -constitutional and that It is a greater value that we must claim for social security. ”

On the adoption of the Social State of Law, said "... Social Security, as a mechanism to achieve the purposes of the Social State of Law, must generate a commitment to the States, which is specified in specific obligations to protect citizens." He also commented that “Social Security is today an indispensable element for the economic and stable development of all countries. Without it, it is very difficult for growth fruits to reach the most unprotected sectors of the population ”

In relation to the protection of certain sectors, the new general secretary pointed out that “despite the importance of social security, we must recognize that in our countries there are broad sectors that do not have adequate protection against the adverse effects that may affect their heritage, their health or their lives. Still the poverty in Latin American are high in terms of international comparisons. What we need to do in this front is much. ”

On the investment in social security of Latin America, said «Latin AmericaIt has been considerably increasing its investment in Social Security as a percentage of its GDP, so it is necessary The necessary coordination that allows the efficiency in the processes and in this way guarantees the satisfaction of citizens. It is necessary to recover the legitimacy of the systems in front of the citizen, improving the efficiency and performance of the security and social protection systems. ”

He highlighted the support that the organization has had by Spain, since its origins (First Ibero -American Social Security Congress held in Madrid in 1951 and its creation as an international body in Lima in 1954), and the relevant collaboration of Social Security Organizations Spaniards and expressly thanked his firm support.

Finally, he said that “caring, consolidating protect, enhancing and strengthening the great legacy that the OISS has built in these 60 years and what today represents for member countries, for its more than 150 institutions and for society in general, is The greatest task I undertake from today, "he clarified:" I assume this challenge with great responsibility, with my ability to work, my experience, my vocation of service, the sense of belonging with this institution that has been my house for more than 15 years and with the love and passion that move me for social issues. "
